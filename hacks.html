    Hacks

    Click on the buttons to learn more about the 3 R's (reduce, reuse, recycle)!

    Reduce!

    • Reducing the amount of waste you produce!
    • You can do this by:
      • Only buying what you need
      • Choosing products with less packaging
      • Buying in bulk
      • Look for items that you can reuse
    • Done at no cost to you!

    Reuse!

    • Reusing items over and over again instead of immediately throwing them out
      • Using reusable bags instead of plastic ones
      • Refilling a water bottle instead of using a plastic one
      • Repurposing items of clothing, or donating them to charity
    • Done at no cost to you!

    Recycle!

    • Recycle items like plastic bottles, glass, cardboard, etc.
      • Make sure to read the recycling label to know how to correctly recycle the item
      • Also make sure which parts of your item can be recycled, and how many times it can be recycled
    • Done at no cost to you!

    Compost


    • Natureâ€™s form of recycling!
    • Feeding the soil using food scraps and other waste
    • Keeping waste out of landfills and decreasing greenhouse gases
    • To compost:
      • Set up a compost bin in partial shade or in the sun.
      • Put 2-3 parts of brown material like fall leaves, wood chips, newspaper clippings, etc, and 1 part green material like vegetable and fruit scraps, coffee grounds, eggshells, etc.
      • Add water to keep compost moist, and turn the pile once a month.

    Water usage

    Decrease water usage to save resources and energy (and reduce water bill)!

    Eat locally-produced and organic food

    Locally-produced food produces fewer transportation emissions and is fresher and good for your body. This is also better for the environment, as these foods usually aren't grown with hormones or pesticides.

    Insulate and seal your home

    Reduce air leaks and stop drafts by using caulk, weather stripping, and insulation to seal your home's envelope. Add more insulation to your attic to block out heat and cold. You can save up to 20% on heating and cooling costs and significantly enhance home comfort with comprehensive sealing and insulating measures. Additonally, you can get federal tax credits for reducint your home's energy use assosiated with heating, cooling, drafts, etc.

    Travel hacks

    While driving, avoid unnecessary braking and acceleration, which can result in 40% more fuel consumption. Additionally, use traffic apps to avoid traffic jams, turn on cruise control for longer trips, and use less air conditioning while driving. Try to avoid flying, but if necessary, fly nonstop and go economy class.

    Measuring carbon footprint!

    Use a carbon footprint calculator to estimate your household greenhouse gas emissions, which can result from energy use, transportation, and waste disposal. This can help you understand where your emissions come from and identify ways to reduce them. It will help save the environment, and lower your energy costs!
