Transportation

There are also many green energy alternatives for transportation! Private transportation options include electric cars, biking, and walking. These forms are energy-efficient and have lower to no carbon emissions. Public transportation helps save overall energy consumption by sharing travel with other people. There are also tax rebate options! Please look at the following cards to learn more about how you can travel greener!


Private Transportation


Walking

  • A person walking 1 mile emits 0 grams of carbon dioxide.
  • Walking helps strengthen bones, lower blood pressure, improve sleep, and burn calories.
  • Burns calories and helps you lose weight and then maintain it.
  • It’s free and saves a lot of energy!

Bike

  • According to research done by University of Oxford, choosing a bike over a car just once a day can reduce the average person’s transportation-related emissions by 67%.
  • Choosing a bike over a car can save you a lot of money over time.
    • Between gas, oil changes, tire rotations, and other maintenance costs, not to mention car insurance or any car payments, you could be spending thousands of dollars a year to own, use, and maintain your car.
  • Also helps with exercise! Takes up minimal energy.

Electric Car

  • Electric vehicles are more efficient in converting energy to power cars and trucks, electricity across the board is cleaner and cheaper as a fuel for vehicles.
  • Running electric or hybrid cars on the grid has lower greenhouse gas emissions than gasoline-powered cars, according to the Union of Concerned Scientists.
  • Tax rebate:
    • Receive an incentive of up to $4,000 when you purchase or lease a new all-electric vehicle in New Jersey.
    • Receive a rebate of up to $250 when you purchase an eligible electric vehicle charger for your home.
    • Zero Emission Vehicles are exempt from the NJ state sales tax, so you’ll save 6.625% on the sale, lease or rental of a new or used battery electric vehicle in weight classes 1-8.




Public Transportation


Bus

  • Public buses greatly lower carbon emissions compared to private vehicles. According to the UITP, every person commuting by public transport reduces their carbon footprint by an average of 16%.
  • Upgrading these systems with energy-efficient technologies, such as electric buses, can further enhance emission reductions.
  • Cost Savings:
    • Using public busing saves money on fuel and parking expenses. A study by the APTA revealed that individuals can save more than $10,000 per year by commuting via public transport instead of driving a car.
    • Investing in energy-efficient public busing infrastructure lessens maintenance costs and enhances long-term affordability for both transport authorities and commuters.

Train

  • Is the least emissions-intensive form of public transportation – it will help reduce overall emissions
  • There is an expansion of the use of electric trains as well, particularly to replace diesel-powered freight trains
  • Rail emissions are currently on average around one-fifth those of air travel. Emissions from electrified passenger rail are even lower, especially when powered by renewables or nuclear power.
  • Tax Rebate:
    • The Federal Government currently allows a pre-tax commuter benefit of $300 per month. That means you could save up to $900/year by not paying federal income tax on that salary.

Ferry

  • Ferries are an accessible, affordable form of public transportation that decreases people’s reliance on cars, cutting their travel expenses and carbon footprint.
  • Sometimes faster than regular transportation
  • Solar powered:
    • Produce zero emissions, reducing carbon footprints and minimizing the impact on marine ecosystems.
    • Although the initial investment in solar-powered vessels may be higher than traditional boats, the long-term savings on fuel costs are substantial. Sunlight, being a renewable energy source, eliminates the need for expensive diesel or gasoline, ensuring significant savings in the long run.
