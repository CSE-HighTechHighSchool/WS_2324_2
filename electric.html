Solar Power

Solar power is a renewable energy source from the sun's light, and is reliable and energy efficient! Consider getting solar panels, or drawing electricity from a solar farm.

Efficient Lighting

Many homes have outdated lighting systems that take up too much energy. Make sure to update your lighting!

Heating&Cooling

Homes with outdated systems often break down and use a lot of energy. Efficient heating and cooling can make homes more comfortable.
  • Solar power converts sunlight into electrical energy in one of two ways:
    • Photovoltaic (PV) panels
      • Used in solar panels, energy from the sunlight is absorbed by the PV cells in the panel.
      • This energy creates electrical charges that move in response to an internal electrical field in the cell, causing electricity to flow.
    • Concentrated solar power
      • Use mirrors to reflect and concentrate sunlight onto receivers that collect and convert it to heat, which is then used to produce electricity or stored for later use.
      • Used in very large power plants.
Here are some things you can do to update your lighting to make it energy efficient!
  • LEDs and CFLs
    • While LEDs are more efficient than CFLs, both help with reducing energy and lasting longer. LEDs use up to 90% less energy and last up to 25 times longer than traditional incandescent bulbs. The prices have also decreased as innovation has went on.
  • Timers and Dimmers
    • Using timers and dimmers can help with reducing the amount of light you use, saving you money and saving the planet’s energy!
  • Tips:
    • Set your thermostat as low as is comfortable in the winter and lower the setpoint when you're sleeping or away from home.
    • Clean filters on furnaces, warm-air registers, baseboard heaters, and radiators, and don’t block them with furniture, carpeting, or drapes.
    • Eliminate trapped air from hot-water radiators once or twice a season
    • Turn off exhaust fans within 20 minutes of cooking or bathing; when replacing exhaust fans, consider installing high-efficiency, low-noise models.
    • During winter, keep the draperies and shades on your south-facing windows open during the day to allow the sunlight to enter your home and closed at night to reduce the chill you may feel from cold windows.

Water usage

Decrease water usage to save resources and energy (and reduce water bill)!

Eat locally-produced and organic food

Locally-produced food produces fewer transportation emissions and is fresher and good for your body. This is also better for the environment, as these foods usually aren't grown with hormones or pesticides.

Insulate and seal your home

Reduce air leaks and stop drafts by using caulk, weather stripping, and insulation to seal your home's envelope. Add more insulation to your attic to block out heat and cold. You can save up to 20% on heating and cooling costs and significantly enhance home comfort with comprehensive sealing and insulating measures. Additonally, you can get federal tax credits for reducint your home's energy use assosiated with heating, cooling, drafts, etc.

Travel hacks

While driving, avoid unnecessary braking and acceleration, which can result in 40% more fuel consumption. Additionally, use traffic apps to avoid traffic jams, turn on cruise control for longer trips, and use less air conditioning while driving. Try to avoid flying, but if necessary, fly nonstop and go economy class.

Measuring carbon footprint!

Use a carbon footprint calculator to estimate your household greenhouse gas emissions, which can result from energy use, transportation, and waste disposal. This can help you understand where your emissions come from and identify ways to reduce them. It will help save the environment, and lower your energy costs!
