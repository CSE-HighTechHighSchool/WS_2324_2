Healthy Modes of Transport!

  • There are also many green energy alternatives for transportation!
  • Private transportation options include electric cars, biking, and walking. These forms are energy-efficient and have lower to no carbon emissions.
  • Public transportation helps save overall energy consumption by sharing travel with other people. There are also tax rebate options!
  • Please look at the following cards to learn more about how you can travel greener!


Personal Transportation


Walking

  • A person walking 1 mile emits 0 grams of carbon dioxide.
  • Walking helps strengthen bones, lower blood pressure, improve sleep, and burn calories.
  • Burns calories and helps you lose weight and then maintain it.
  • It’s free and saves a lot of energy!

Bike

  • According to research done by University of Oxford, choosing a bike over a car just once a day can reduce the average person’s transportation-related emissions by 67%.
  • Choosing a bike over a car can save you a lot of money over time.
    • Between gas, oil changes, tire rotations, and other maintenance costs, not to mention car insurance or any car payments, you could be spending thousands of dollars a year to own, use, and maintain your car.
  • Also helps with exercise! Takes up minimal energy.

Electric Car

  • Electric vehicles are more efficient in converting energy to power cars and trucks, electricity across the board is cleaner and cheaper as a fuel for vehicles.
  • Running electric or hybrid cars on the grid has lower greenhouse gas emissions than gasoline-powered cars, according to the Union of Concerned Scientists.
  • Tax rebate:
    • Receive an incentive of up to $4,000 when you purchase or lease a new all-electric vehicle in New Jersey.
    • Receive a rebate of up to $250 when you purchase an eligible electric vehicle charger for your home.
    • Zero Emission Vehicles are exempt from the NJ state sales tax, so you’ll save 6.625% on the sale, lease or rental of a new or used battery electric vehicle in weight classes 1-8.




Public Transportation


Bus

  • Greatly lowers carbon emissions compared to cars. People commuting by public transport reduce their carbon footprint by an average of 16%.
  • Upgrading these systems with energy-efficient ones, such as electric buses, can further enhance emission reductions.
  • Cost Savings:
    • Saves money on fuel and parking expenses. You can save over $10,000 per year by commuting via public transport.
    • Investing in energy-efficient busing lessens maintenance costs and enhances long-term affordability.

Train

  • Is the least emissions-intensive form of public transportation
  • There is an expansion of the use of electric trains to replace diesel-powered freight trains
  • Rail emissions are around 1/5 those of air travel. Emissions from electrified passenger rail are even lower, especially when powered by renewables or nuclear power.
  • Tax Rebate:
    • The Federal Government allows a pre-tax commuter benefit of $300 per month. That means you could save up to $900/year by not paying federal income tax on that salary.

Ferry

  • Accessible, affordable form of public transportation that decreases people’s reliance on cars, cutting their travel expenses and carbon footprint.
  • Sometimes faster than regular transportation
  • Solar powered:
    • Produce zero emissions, reducing carbon footprints and minimizing the impact on marine ecosystems.
    • Substantial long-term savings on fuel costs. Sunlight, being a renewable energy source, eliminates the need for expensive diesel or gasoline.
