Solar Power

  • Solar power converts sunlight into electrical energy in one of two ways:
    • Photovoltaic (PV) panels
      • Used in solar panels, energy from the sunlight is absorbed by the PV cells in the panel.
      • This energy creates electrical charges that move in response to an internal electrical field in the cell, causing electricity to flow.
    • Concentrated solar power
      • Use mirrors to reflect and concentrate sunlight onto receivers that collect and convert it to heat, which is then used to produce electricity or stored for later use.
      • Used in very large power plants.

Solar Power Incentives

Federal Tax Benefit

  • Benefit from the 30% Investment Tax Credit when you set up a system with at least 2.3 kwh capacity.
  • On average, homeowners save $10,789 through this incentive.

State SREC-2 Initiative

  • Guaranteed a fixed rate for 15 years: $90 for every 1,000 kw your solar system generates.
  • Savings of over $13,500 across the system's lifespan!

Net Energy Metering (NEM)

  • Your power provider bills you only for the electricity consumed beyond your system's generation.
  • If your solar setup contributes more power to the grid than you use, you'll earn credit on your utility invoice.
